Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS)

ASTCT Grading Criteria

Grade 1
Fever ≥38°C
No hypotension, no hypoxia
Grade 2
Fever + Hypotension
OR Hypoxia requiring low-flow O₂ (≤6L/min)
Grade 3
Fever + Hypotension requiring vasopressors
OR Hypoxia requiring high-flow O₂ or BiPAP
Grade 4
Life-threatening
Vasopressors + mechanical ventilation

Product-Specific Insights

Axicabtagene ciloleucel: Highest CRS rates (93% any grade), early onset (day 2), associated with robust CAR-T expansion. CD28 costimulation may contribute to rapid, intense cytokine release.
Tisagenlecleucel: Moderate CRS rates (58% any grade), slightly delayed onset (day 3). 4-1BB costimulation associated with more controlled inflammatory response.
Lisocabtagene maraleucel: Lowest CRS rates (42% any grade), earliest onset (day 1) but shorter duration. Controlled IL-6 release profile may contribute to manageable toxicity.

Product-Specific Insights

Axicabtagene ciloleucel: Highest ICANS rates (64% any grade, 28% grade ≥3). Often concurrent with CRS. Median onset day 4-5, can be prolonged and severe.
Tisagenlecleucel: Lower ICANS rates (21% any grade, 7.4% grade ≥3). Less frequent concurrent CRS. May have delayed onset relative to CRS.
Lisocabtagene maraleucel: Moderate ICANS rates (30% any grade, 10% grade ≥3). Balanced toxicity profile with manageable neurotoxicity.

Risk Factors for Severe Toxicities

High-Risk Factors for Severe CRS

  • High tumor burden: Elevated LDH, large tumor mass, high MTV
  • Inflammatory state: Elevated CRP, ferritin, IL-6
  • Product type: CD28-based CARs (axi-cel) > 4-1BB CARs
  • CAR-T expansion: High peak expansion correlates with CRS severity
  • Age: Younger patients may have more robust inflammatory responses
  • Thrombocytopenia: Platelet count <100K associated with severe CRS

High-Risk Factors for Severe ICANS

  • Concurrent severe CRS: Grade ≥3 CRS increases ICANS risk
  • Peak CAR-T levels: Very high expansion associated with neurotoxicity
  • CNS involvement: History of CNS lymphoma
  • Age extremes: Very young or elderly patients
  • Baseline neurologic issues: Prior CNS infections, seizures
  • Bridging therapy: CNS-penetrating agents may increase risk

ICANS Management

Grade 1
Monitoring:
• Neurologic assessments q8h
• ICE score monitoring
• Seizure precautions
Grade 2
Corticosteroids:
• Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6h
• Neurologic monitoring
• MRI if worsening
Grade 3-4
High-dose steroids:
• Methylprednisolone 1000mg/day
• Anti-seizure prophylaxis
• Intensive monitoring

Key Management Principles

Timing is critical: Early recognition and intervention improve outcomes. CRS typically precedes ICANS.

Tocilizumab considerations: Crosses blood-brain barrier poorly; may not prevent ICANS. Can be repeated if needed.

Corticosteroid use: Reserved for ICANS or refractory CRS. May impact CAR-T persistence, but life-saving when needed.

Product differences: Understanding product-specific toxicity profiles helps anticipate and prepare for management needs.
